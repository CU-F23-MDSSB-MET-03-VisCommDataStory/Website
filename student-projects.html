1 Milestone 1: Choose data and topic ‚òëÔ∏è

The student project report is the central assessment item of the module for Examination and Grading.

Projects can be done in teams of two or individually.

Project pitches (see below) shall be done during class the latest on Week 7: Tue, Oct 18, better in the weeks before, because you save more time for the actual work.

To do until Sat, Oct 1:

  • Think about the topic of your project (see the list for the pitch below) and form a team.
  • Decide when you want to pitch your project (Week 5, Oct 4; Week 6, Oct 11; Week 7, Oct 18), and write it on Teams in the course‚Äôs general channel as a reply to the respective conversation
Tip

Individuals or teams which settled on project idea can start working on the project pitch!
The earlier you start the more time you have to work on the actual project. The project pitch is a short intro in class (5-10 min.) which tells:

  • The (main) data source you use (and have looked at already)
  • The main points/questions/insights the data story should be about
  • One draft visualization of the data
  • Optional: Further ideas for the project, questions you are pondering
Important

You can make your data story into a nice thing to show your competence to the outside world. It is not too difficult to publish a website from your final repository. Make your report such that it is worth to publish and show it!

2 Milestone 2: Storyframing ‚ÄºüìäüìàüìâÔ∏è

Before you can communicate insights with a data story, you must find one!

You have done the storyframing when:

  • You have your data operational on your computer to prepare slidedeck and report.
  • You have selected the main dimensions and the key metrics you want to focus on in the data.
  • You know the main insight(s) you want to communicate.
  • You have an idea of the audience you want to address.

Activity:

  1. Work on data import, a data description, and a first visualization in your document. Use the template in index.qmd in your project repository https://github.com/JU-F22-MDSSB-MET-03/project-YOURNAMES.
  2. Meet with the instructor: Discuss your storyframing. Appointments lie in the regular class hours, so they have been fixed here https://github.com/JU-F22-MDSSB-MET-03/Schedule-Organization.
